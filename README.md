# Company-Inventory-Data-Analysis-Project
This project focuses on inventory and vendor performance analysis using Python to evaluate sales trends, profit margins, and inventory turnover. Statistical techniques such as 95% confidence intervals and hypothesis testing were applied to compare profitability between top-performing and low-performing vendors.

Inventory Data Analysis Project
Project Overview

This project analyzes inventory and vendor performance data to uncover operational inefficiencies and support better business decision-making. Using Python-based data analysis, the project evaluates vendor performance, inventory turnover, capital locked in stock, and profitability differences between vendor groups.

The analysis focuses on identifying slow-moving inventory, high-performing brands, and vendors contributing to excess stock. Statistical techniques such as confidence intervals and hypothesis testing are applied to determine whether observed differences in vendor profitability are statistically significant.

Business Problems Addressed
1. Vendor Performance Analysis

Identify top-performing vendors and brands based on profit margins and sales performance.

Determine vendors contributing the most to overall sales and profitability.

2. Slow Moving Inventory Detection

Identify vendors with low inventory turnover, which indicates excess stock and slow-moving products.

3. Capital Locked in Inventory

Calculate how much capital is locked in unsold inventory per vendor.

Determine which vendor contributes the highest inventory holding cost.

4. Profitability vs Sales Analysis

Identify brands that have high profit margins but low sales performance, indicating opportunities for:

Promotional campaigns

Pricing adjustments

Marketing improvements

5. Statistical Analysis of Vendor Profitability

Compute 95% confidence intervals for profit margins of:

Top-performing vendors

Low-performing vendors

Perform a Two-Sample T-Test to evaluate whether the difference in profit margins between the two groups is statistically significant.

Hypothesis:

H₀ (Null Hypothesis): There is no significant difference in the mean profit margins of top-performing and low-performing vendors.

H₁ (Alternative Hypothesis): The mean profit margins of top-performing and low-performing vendors are significantly different.
